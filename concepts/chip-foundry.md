## What is a Chip Foundry?

A chip foundry, also known as a semiconductor foundry, is a factory that manufactures integrated circuits (ICs) for other companies. These companies may design the chips themselves, or they may license designs from other sources. The foundry then takes these designs and uses its specialized equipment and processes to fabricate the chips on silicon wafers. This article covers the definition of a **chip foundry** and its role in the semiconductor industry.

## How a Chip Foundry Manufactures Semiconductors: A Step-by-Step Overview

The semiconductor manufacturing process is incredibly complex, involving hundreds of steps and sophisticated equipment. Here's a simplified overview: 1. **Wafer Fabrication:** This involves growing a large, cylindrical ingot of silicon, which is then sliced into thin wafers. 2. **Photolithography:** A light-sensitive material (photoresist) is applied to the wafer. A mask containing the circuit design is used to expose specific areas to ultraviolet light. 3. **Etching:** The exposed areas of the photoresist are removed. The underlying silicon is etched away, creating the desired patterns on the wafer. 4. **Deposition:** Thin layers of various materials, such as metals and insulators, are deposited onto the wafer to build up the different layers of the integrated circuit.

5. **Ion Implantation:** Ions are implanted into the silicon to modify its electrical properties. 6. **Metallization:** Metal layers are deposited and patterned to create the interconnections between the different components of the circuit. 7. **Testing:** Each chip on the wafer is tested to ensure it meets the required specifications. 8.
6. **Dicing:** The wafer is cut into individual chips. 9.

**Packaging:** Each chip is packaged to protect it and provide electrical connections to the outside world. ## Chip Foundry Models: Pure-Play vs Integrated (IDMs)

The chip foundry market splits into two models with distinct economics. Pure-play foundries like TSMC control 60% of global wafer production and earned $87B in 2025 revenue ([SemiAnalysis](https://www.semianalysis.com/)). Integrated manufacturers like Intel and Samsung combine design and fabrication, investing $20-50B annually per fab. There are two main types of companies in the semiconductor manufacturing industry: pure-play foundries and integrated device manufacturers (IDMs).

* **Pure-Play Foundries:** These companies, like TSMC and GlobalFoundries, focus exclusively on manufacturing chips for other companies. They do not design or sell their own chips.
* **Integrated Device Manufacturers (IDMs):** These companies, like Intel and Samsung, both design and manufacture their own chips. They may also offer foundry services to other companies, but their primary focus is on manufacturing their own products.

The pure-play foundry model has several advantages. It also reduces the capital investment required to enter the semiconductor industry, as companies can focus on either design or manufacturing, but not both.

* **Moore's Law:** The historical trend of doubling the number of transistors on a chip every two years is becoming increasingly difficult and expensive to maintain.
