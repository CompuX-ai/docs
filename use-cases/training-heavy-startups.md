## The Challenge: High Compute Costs for AI Training

For AI startups, training AI models can take up a large part of their money. This hurts growth and new ideas. Many new companies spend 20% to 50% of their money on compute. This limits their ability to try different models, improve the ones they have, and launch their products. Getting enough GPU resources is also hard because demand is often higher than supply. Regular cloud providers can be costly and not flexible, making it hard for startups to manage compute costs.

The need for GPU compute is growing fast. This is because AI models are getting more complex, and more data is used for training. AI compute requirements surged 10x from 2020 to 2025 ([Epoch AI](https://epochai.org/data/epochdb/notable-ai-models)), driven by larger models and broader adoption. This big increase has caused a shortage of GPUs and higher prices. Training large language models (LLMs) can cost tens of thousands to millions of dollars. As AI models get better, it is very important to have compute tools that are affordable and scalable.

| Budget Constraints | AI startups often have small budgets, making it hard to pay for compute resources. | Limits model complexity, reduces training, impacts model performance. |
